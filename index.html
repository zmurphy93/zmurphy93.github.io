About Me

Welcome! I am an operational meteorologist, forecasting the weather in the Northeast United States. I received my M.S. in Atmospheric Sciences from the University at Albany, SUNY and a B.S. in Atmospheric Sciences and a Certificate in Mathematics from the University of Wisconsin - Madison. Intensive scientific research and experience in data analysis were integral components to these degrees. When I am not busy agonizing over the weather forecast, I am actively applying programming and data analysis techniques to projects of my own personal interest.

In the process of obtaining my degrees, the complex datasets that are used to perform scientific research in this field have necessitated my development of programming and data processing skills. As a result, I have created a few projects that have allowed me to further my skills in machine learning, data analysis, and statistical analysis. Many of those projects shown here use meteorology as an inspiration.

Feel free to take a peek at some of my projects above and ask me any questions!
