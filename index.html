ABOUT ZACH

Meteorologist by day (even sometimes at night!), budding software developer and data geek by night.

I was born in Illinois and raised in Wisconsin where cheese and bratwursts reign supreme. I left my small town to become a badger at the University of Wisconsin - Madison and obtained a degree in Atmospheric Sciences, as well as a certificate in Mathematics. In 2015, I left the Midwest and moved to Albany, New York to pursue my M.S. in Atmospheric Sciences.

During my time in graduate school, I recognized the importance of computer technology and began working on my data analysis, statistics, and software development skills. As a result, I developed a love and passion for the fields of software development and data science, even before knowing what they were. Python became my best friend during this time and it remains a favorite tool of mine in a variety of applications.

In 2017, I moved to the Lehigh Valley in Pennsylvania, where I work as a consultant meteorologist just across the border in New Jersey. I work on a variety of software and web development projects when I am not forecasting. I am passionate about developing web applications that display scientific data and am fascinated by the rapidly emerging field of machine learning and its applications.

Heat Wave Identification


Project Type: Data Science Study

Reanalysis data and daily temperature records from Chicago are used to predict the occurrence of anomalously hot days in Chicago. Anomalously hot days were identified by using any day where the maximum temperature exceeded the 95th precentile for that day's high temperatures. Several classifiers were trained on various subsets of features to determine a relationship between commonly used meteorological variables and the occurrence of anomalously hot weather. Future opportunities are also explored with the results of this study.

Technologies Used: Pandas, Scikit-Learn, Xarray, Metpy, Python, Jupyter
