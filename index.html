ABOUT ZACH

Meteorologist by day (even sometimes at night!), budding software developer and data geek by night.

I was born in Illinois and raised in Wisconsin where cheese and bratwursts reign supreme. I left my small town to become a badger at the University of Wisconsin - Madison and obtained a degree in Atmospheric Sciences, as well as a certificate in Mathematics. In 2015, I left the Midwest and moved to Albany, New York to pursue my M.S. in Atmospheric Sciences.

During my time in graduate school, I recognized the importance of computer technology and began working on my data analysis, statistics, and software development skills to improve my data analysis skills. Python became my best friend during this time and it remains a favorite tool of mine in a variety of applications.

In 2017, I moved to the Lehigh Valley in Pennsylvania, where I work as a consultant meteorologist just across the border in New Jersey. I work on a variety of software and web development projects when I am not forecasting. I am passionate about developing web applications that display scientific data and am fascinated by unifying meteorological problems and machine learning.
